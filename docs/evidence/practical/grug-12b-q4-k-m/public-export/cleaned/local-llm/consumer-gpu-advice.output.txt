### Practical Model Selection for 12GB VRAM

With 12GB of VRAM, your goal is to find the "sweet spot" where a model is smart enough to be useful but small enough to run fast and leave room for context.

#### 1. Why VRAM Headroom Matters
VRAM is not just for the model weights; it is also used for the **KV Cache** (the memory needed to "remember" the current conversation).
*   **The Risk:** If a model takes up 11.5GB of your 12GB, you only have 0.5GB left for context. This leads to "Out of Memory" (OOM) errors as soon as the conversation gets long.
*   **The Strategy:** Aim for a model that fits comfortably (e.g., 8-9GB) so you have 3-4GB of headroom for a large context window.

#### 2. Speed vs. Quality
Speed is a function of parameter count and quantization; quality is a function of training data and architecture.
*   **Speed:** A 7B model will generate tokens much faster than a 70B model.
*   **Quality:** A 70B model might follow complex instructions better, but if it runs at 1 token per second, it is not "useful" for interactive tasks.
*   **The Balance:** A model that is "fast enough" to be usable is often better than a "high quality" model that is too slow to interact with.

#### 3. Small/Useful vs. Large/Awkward
A smaller model that is well-tuned for a specific task (e.g., coding or summarization) often outperforms a massive, general-purpose model that is "awkward" (slow, prone to hallucination, or inconsistent).
*   **Example:** A 14B model optimized for reasoning may outperform a 70B model that is heavily quantized (compressed) to fit on your hardware.

#### 4. Recommended Testing Order
Do not jump to the largest model that fits. Start small to establish a baseline:

1.  **Baseline (8B - 14B):** Test a high-quality 8B or 14B model (e.g., Llama 3.1 8B or Mistral NeMo 12B). These are designed to fit comfortably in 12GB with room for context.
2.  **Specialized (14B - 30B):** If the 8B model is not smart enough, move to a 14B or 30B model with a higher quantization (e.g., Q4_K_M).
3.  **Large (70B+):** Only move to 70B models if you are okay with significant quantization (e.g., Q2_K or Q3_K_L) and slower speeds.

#### 5. Actionable Advice
*   **Quantization is your friend:** Use GGUF or EXL2 formats to fit larger models into your 12GB.
*   **Check the math:** Before downloading, check the model's size. A 14B model at Q4_K_M is roughly 9GB. This leaves 3GB for context.
*   **Start with 8B/14B:** These are the most practical starting points for 12GB hardware.
